About Olympics

  • The Olympic Games are the world’s foremost sporting event, bringing together athletes from all corners of the globe to compete in a spirit of excellence, friendship, and respect. Originating in ancient Greece over 2,700 years ago, the modern Olympic Games were revived in 1896 and have since become a symbol of unity and global harmony.

  • Held every four years, the Summer and Winter Olympics showcase a wide range of sports, from athletics and swimming to skiing and ice hockey. Athletes compete not just for medals, but for honor, national pride, and the pursuit of personal and collective greatness.

  • The Olympic values — excellence, respect, and friendship — lie at the heart of every event, promoting fair play and inspiring millions worldwide. With each edition, the Olympics continue to transcend barriers, celebrate diversity, and leave a lasting legacy of hope and inspiration.
