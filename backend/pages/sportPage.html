Basketball has become one of the most electrifying and followed events in the Summer Olympics, blending speed, skill, and strategy on a global stage. Since its official inclusion in 1936, the sport has evolved tremendously, with powerhouse nations like the United States setting the standard through generations of dominant performances. The Dream Team of 1992 redefined Olympic basketball, showcasing NBA talent on the world stage and inspiring global competitiveness. Women's basketball has also flourished, with teams like the USA displaying consistent excellence. Over the decades, countries across Europe, Asia, and the Americas have risen to challenge traditional dominance, turning Olympic basketball into a true celebration of international talent, unity, and competitive spirit.
